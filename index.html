In 1972 American psychologists, Paul Ekman and Wallace V. Friesen identified six basic emotions based on studying the isolated culture of
people from the Fori tribe in Papua New Guinea. Overall,they were able to identify these six emotions: Anger, Disgust, Fear, Happines, Sadness, Surprise.
